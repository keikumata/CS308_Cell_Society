1 # cellsociety
2 ### Rules to create xml files
3 Each simulation has an assigned ID. Schelling is 1, Spreading Fire is 2, Wa-tor is 3, Game of Life is 4. The simulation world is a square, and the user must specify how many cells are on each side, and the frames per seconds (generations per second) of the simulation.
4 
5 Each simulation requires different amount of parameters. Detailed examples for each simulation is listed below.
6 ### XML Examples
7 #### Schelling:
8 ```
9 <sim>
10 <type>1</type> (specify the ID of the simulation)
11 <length>30</length> (amount of cells on each side of the simulation world)
12 <fps>10</fps> (generations per second)
13 <params>
14 <param>40</param> (percentage of blue cells)
15 <param>40</param> (percentage of red cells)
16 <param>70</param> (threshold for satisfaction)
17 </params>
18 </sim>
19 ```
